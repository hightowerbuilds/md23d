# Calculus

The study of continuous change.

## The Derivative

$$
\frac{d}{dx}\left[f(x)g(x)\right] = f'(x)g(x) + f(x)g'(x)
$$

The product rule: differentiation of a product of two functions.

$$
\lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f(x+h) - f(x)}{h}
$$

The limit definition of the derivative — the instantaneous rate of change.

## Integration

$$
\int_a^b f(x)\,dx = F(b) - F(a)
$$

The Fundamental Theorem of Calculus connects differentiation and integration.

$$
\int u\,dv = uv - \int v\,du
$$

Integration by parts — derived from the product rule, reversed.

## The Gaussian Integral

$$
\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} e^{-x^2}\,dx = \sqrt{\pi}
$$

One of the most beautiful results in all of mathematics.

## Taylor Series

$$
f(x) = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{f^{(n)}(a)}{n!}(x-a)^n
$$

Any smooth function can be expressed as an infinite polynomial about a point.

## Euler's Identity

$$
e^{i\pi} + 1 = 0
$$

Five fundamental constants. One equation.

## Chain Rule

$$
\frac{d}{dx}\left[f(g(x))\right] = f'(g(x)) \cdot g'(x)
$$

## Cauchy's Integral Formula

$$
f(z_0) = \frac{1}{2\pi i} \oint_\gamma \frac{f(z)}{z - z_0}\,dz
$$

The value of a complex function anywhere inside a contour is determined entirely by its values on the contour.
