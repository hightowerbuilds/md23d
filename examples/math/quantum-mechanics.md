# Quantum Mechanics

The physics of the very small — where probability replaces certainty.

## The Schrödinger Equation

$$
i\hbar\frac{\partial}{\partial t}\Psi(\mathbf{r},t) = \hat{H}\Psi(\mathbf{r},t)
$$

The fundamental equation governing how quantum states evolve in time.

## Time-Independent Form

$$
\hat{H}\psi = E\psi
$$

When the Hamiltonian does not depend on time, energy eigenstates emerge.

## The Uncertainty Principle

$$
\sigma_x \sigma_p \geq \frac{\hbar}{2}
$$

Position and momentum cannot both be known precisely at the same time.

## Born Rule

$$
P(x) = |\Psi(x,t)|^2
$$

The probability of finding a particle at position x is the squared modulus of the wave function.

## Commutator

$$
[\hat{x},\,\hat{p}] = i\hbar
$$

Position and momentum operators do not commute — the root of uncertainty.

## Dirac Notation

$$
\langle \phi | \hat{A} | \psi \rangle = \int \phi^*(x)\, \hat{A}\, \psi(x)\, dx
$$

The expectation value of observable A in state ψ, evaluated against state φ.

## Hydrogen Atom Energy Levels

$$
E_n = -\frac{13.6\,\text{eV}}{n^2}, \quad n = 1, 2, 3, \ldots
$$

The quantized energy levels of the hydrogen atom.

## Path Integral (Feynman)

$$
\langle x_f | e^{-i\hat{H}T/\hbar} | x_i \rangle = \int \mathcal{D}[x(t)]\, e^{iS[x]/\hbar}
$$

All possible paths contribute — each weighted by a phase equal to the classical action.
