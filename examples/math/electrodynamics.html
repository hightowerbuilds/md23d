Classical Electrodynamics

Maxwell's equations govern all classical electromagnetic phenomena.

Maxwell's Equations (Differential Form)

Gauss's law for electric fields:

\nabla \cdot \mathbf{E} = \frac{\rho}{\varepsilon_0}

Gauss's law for magnetic fields — no magnetic monopoles:

\nabla \cdot \mathbf{B} = 0

Faraday's law of induction:

\nabla \times \mathbf{E} = -\frac{\partial \mathbf{B}}{\partial t}

Ampère–Maxwell law:

\nabla \times \mathbf{B} = \mu_0\mathbf{J} + \mu_0\varepsilon_0\frac{\partial \mathbf{E}}{\partial t}

The Lorentz Force

\mathbf{F} = q\left(\mathbf{E} + \mathbf{v} \times \mathbf{B}\right)

The force on a charged particle moving through electromagnetic fields.

Speed of Light

c = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\mu_0\varepsilon_0}}

Maxwell derived the speed of light from the constants of electricity and magnetism — one of the greatest unifications in physics.

Wave Equation

\nabla^2\mathbf{E} - \frac{1}{c^2}\frac{\partial^2 \mathbf{E}}{\partial t^2} = 0

Poynting Vector

\mathbf{S} = \frac{1}{\mu_0}\left(\mathbf{E} \times \mathbf{B}\right)

The energy flux density of an electromagnetic field — power per unit area.

Electromagnetic Energy Density

u = \frac{1}{2}\left(\varepsilon_0 E^2 + \frac{B^2}{\mu_0}\right)

Larmor Radiation Formula

P = \frac{q^2 a^2}{6\pi\varepsilon_0 c^3}

Power radiated by an accelerating point charge.
